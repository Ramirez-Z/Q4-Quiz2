Photo Gallery of the Philippines


The Philippines is full of tourist attractions across all of its 7,641 islands. They are all equally beautiful and unique in their own right. But you don't know where to start? Well, here are some of the iconic tourist across Luzon, Visayas and Mindanao.



Ifuago, Northern Luzon

Bananue Rice Terraces


The Banaue Rice Terraces are among the Philippines' top tourist destinations. Called the 8th Wonder of the Modern World by locals, these terraces are said to have been carved into the mountains and hillsides by the ancestors of the local indigenous Ifugao tribes.

Hugduan Rice Terraces


The most popular terraces in Hungduan are Hapao Rice Terraces, the Bacung Spider Web Terraces, and the Dakkitan Rice Terraces. Abundant brooks and rivers from the mountains provide water to these terraces all year round. They yield premium heirloom rice varieties.

Mt. Nagchayan


Mt. Nagchayan rises to more than 1,455 MASL and is one of the rustic Ifugao tourist spots. It was a battleground during the Japanese invasion. Both the locals and the Americans pursued relentless drives until the Japanese finally surrendered.

Tentog Falls


Tenogtog falls is a three-layered fall with pools in between. According to locals, Tenogtog is an Ifugao word which means, “chopped.” The falls is named as such because this is where hunters used to chop the meat of deer or wild pigs.





Bicol, Souther Luzon

Mayon Volcano


What is the description of Mayon Volcano? The Mayon Volcano is situated in the southern part of Luzon. Towering at a height of 2,462 MASL, it is known for its perfectly conical shape whose natural beauty has inspired a number of legends and art.

Calaguas Islands


The Calaguas Islands are located in Vinzons,Camarines Norte. It is known for its pristine beaches—the most popular among these is the Mahabang Buhangin beach, which is made up of a long stretch of powdery white sand.

Sumlang Lake


Sumlang Lake offers a peaceful escape from tourist crowds, providing unobstructed 360-degree views of Mayon Volcano. The serene destination is known for its tranquil ambiance and offers a relaxing experience for visitors.

Mount Bulusan


Mount Bulusan is a captivating active volcano in the Philippines, standing at 1,565 meters. It boasts a beautiful crater lake, surrounded by lush forests, and is known for occasional eruptions, making it a popular destination for nature enthusiasts.





Visayas

Chocolate Hills (Bohol)


The Chocolate Hills form a rolling terrain of haycock-shaped hills—mounds of a generally conical and almost symmetrical shape. These cone-shaped or dome-shaped hills are made of grass-covered limestone.

Islas de Gigantes (Iloilo)


Islas de Gigantes in Carles is a stunning group of islands known for their clear blue waters, white sand beaches, etc. This popular tourist spot's name came from the local tales that bones of giant humans were found in one of the caves here.

Moalboal Sardine Run (Cebu)


Moalboal Sardine Run diving packages are widely popular both in the Philippines and abroad. It is comparable to the famous sardine run in South Africa. This is more accessible and consistent compared to the African one.

White Beach Boracay (Aklan)


White Beach Boracay is one of the top tourist spots and the most popular among Visayas beaches. Stretching for 4 kilometers long, featuring very fine white sand and clear blue waters. This is the reason why Boracay has become famous around the world.





Mindanao

Maria Cristiana Falls (Iligan)


The Maria Cristina Falls are often referred to as 'twin falls' as the flow of water is separated by a rock at the brink of the fall. Aside from its natural beauty, this 320-foot-high waterfall provides a part of the electricity supply of Mindanao's Hydroelectric Plant.

Pink Beach (Zamboanga)


The Great Santa Cruz Island is also known as the Pink Beach. It offers an amazing pink sand that results from pulverized red organ pipe coral washed up from offshore mixes with white sand. The blue and green shades of the sea adds more beauty to the island.

Enchanted River (Surigao del Sur)


The Hinatuan Enchanted River in Mindanao, Philippines, is a flawless saltwater river that flows into the Pacific Ocean. it's flow attracts visitors who want to experience the beautiful waters that locals have long thought to be miraculous.

Magpupungko Rock Pools (Siargao)


The Magpupungko Pools near the town of Pilar are natural pools between the rocks and reefs right at the beach, which are only completely visible in low tide, exposing the surrounding rocks alongside beautiful corals and fishes- perfect for snorkeling.
